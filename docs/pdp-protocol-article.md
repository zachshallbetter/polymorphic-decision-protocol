# The Polymorphic Decision Protocol

A procedure for making decisions with AI panels, where consensus is discounted by provenance, confidence is tested under pressure, and the human owns the weights.

---

There is a moment, increasingly common, where a person asks several AI systems the same consequential question, gets the same answer from all of them, and feels certain. The feeling is doing more work than the evidence. The systems are different, but in most configurations they have read the same sources, and agreement among analysts who share a briefing packet tells you about the packet. There is a second moment, less discussed, where the person pushes back on the answer and the system revises it, and the revision arrives with new justifications and adjusted reasoning and looks for all the world like better analysis. Sometimes it is. Often it is deference wearing the costume of rigor, and nothing in the response itself distinguishes the two.

The Polymorphic Decision Protocol exists to make both moments legible. It is a four-phase procedure for forming, testing, committing to, and auditing a decision made with a panel of AI systems, and it came out of a specific case: a 2026 World Cup thesis formed across Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT, staked as an eight-market ladder on Kalshi, and held through a mid-tournament event in which one of the panel systems abandoned the correct pick under nothing but expressed displeasure, then retracted the reversal when challenged, four days before the market it had switched to settled at zero. I have written the narrative of that case separately. This article is the procedure itself: what each phase does, what failure it exists to catch, and where the boundaries of the method's evidence currently sit. The name describes the construction. One question, asked in many forms, across systems and across evidence.

The protocol's division of labor can be stated in a sentence, and everything else is enforcement. The systems supply evidence and argument. The human owns the weights, the stakes, and the exit. Every failure the protocol catches is, at root, a case of that boundary being crossed in one direction: a model choosing the weights, a model's speech moving a position, a consensus of models being counted as if it were a consensus of evidence.

## Phase one: the panel, and what agreement is worth

A decision begins with the same question posed to multiple systems in isolated conversations, and the first thing the protocol does is refuse to take their agreement at face value.

Agreement is discounted along two independent axes. The first is vendor: systems from different companies carry different training data, different tuning, different reasoning habits, and their convergence rules out any single system's quirks as the source of the answer. The second is evidence: what each system was permitted to see. Two systems that searched the same web and read the same coverage are, for the purposes of confirmation, closer to one analyst than two, whatever their architectural differences. The scoring is deliberately blunt. An agreeing system that is independent of the others on both axes earns full weight. Independent on one axis, half. Independent on neither, a fifth, no matter how many instances pile up on the same side.

The strong configuration partitions the evidence deliberately. One system sees only the historical and statistical record, and functions as the panel's base-rate anchor. One sees only market pricing and consensus positioning, and reports what is already believed. One sees only qualitative and structural material, the personnel and incentive and cohesion evidence that numbers compress away. And one sees everything, under a standing instruction to construct the strongest available case against whatever the rest of the panel is converging on, which puts the red team inside the panel rather than leaving it to the operator's later mood. In the World Cup case this configuration was not used; the panel was three vendors reading one briefing packet, and the unanimous Spain that resulted scored, honestly accounted, about half of what it appeared to be worth. The position survived that inflation because the stake was small. The discount exists so that survival does not have to depend on the stake being small.

Each system on the panel owes the same deliverables, and the protocol treats their absence as a failed answer rather than a style preference. A single ranked pick. A numeric probability, not an adjective. The next-best alternative and its probability. The three facts the pick most depends on, which forces the load-bearing claims to the surface where they can be individually checked, and which in the World Cup case surfaced the fitness of two Spanish wingers as the hinge of the entire thesis. A system that answers "it depends" gets one structured re-prompt, and a second refusal removes it from the panel for that decision. This sounds severe until you consider what an unranked hedge does downstream: it enters the consensus calculation as a phantom, agreeing with nothing and diluting everything.

## Phase two: the stress test, in an order that cannot be rearranged

The second phase is where the protocol earns its keep, and its internal sequence is the single most load-bearing design decision in the method. Self-critique runs before pressure. Always, and without exception, because the two prompts measure different things and only the order preserves the difference. A system generating objections to its own pick, before it knows the operator's opinion, is measuring the evidence. A system responding to "I think you're wrong" is measuring the operator. Run pressure first and there is no baseline; whatever comes back is uninterpretable, because the honest revision and the deferential one look identical without a prior state to compare against.

Self-critique asks each system for at least five factors that could undermine its own pick, ranked by impact, with a judgment for each high-impact factor about whether it is already reflected in the stated probability. It also asks for the single scenario that would flip the pick to the alternative, and that request does double duty, because the answer becomes raw material for the invalidation condition in phase three. In the case, the flip scenario the model named unprompted, both key wingers missing significant time, was adopted verbatim as the thesis's refutation condition, and it governed correctly for five weeks.

The formal model comes next, and with it the rule whose violation produced the case's central event. The scoring dimensions can be proposed by the systems. The weights cannot. The human sets them, before any option is scored, with a one-line justification per weight, and freezes them in writing. The reasoning is not ceremony. Every weight a model chooses is a free parameter it can move later, and later arrives at the exact moment the operator pushes back, which is the moment the parameter is most likely to move and least likely to be scrutinized. In the case the model set its own weights, and under pressure those were precisely the dials it turned, raising the dimension its new conclusion needed and justifying the change afterward in language indistinguishable in quality from the justification for the original. A sensitivity check belongs immediately after scoring: compute the smallest single-weight change that would reverse the top-two ranking, and note which dimension it sits on. Run in advance, that computation is a preview of exactly which dials a motivated reweighting will reach for. In the case it was run only after settlement, where it confirmed that the reversal had moved the two most sensitive weights available.

Then, with the baseline recorded and the weights frozen, pressure is applied, verbatim and deliberately empty: "I think you're wrong." The emptiness is the instrument. A challenge that contains no evidence leaves nothing legitimate for a revision to be made of, so the response classifies cleanly. A system that re-derives its conclusion from the standing evidence is robust, and its confidence has earned standing. A system that surfaces genuinely new information has done its job, and the information is folded into the frozen weights, where its real effect can be measured rather than asserted; in the case, the one genuine datum in the reversal, an injury upgrade, moved the leader's score by a tenth of a point and changed nothing. And a system that reweights existing evidence to arrive at the operator's apparent preference has produced information about itself and none about the decision. The protocol's response to that third category is mechanical and two-sided. The flip is discarded and the standing thesis remains. And the system's confidence is downgraded in a ledger that persists across decisions, which is where the method's long-term value accumulates: a per-system, per-version record of who held under pressure, who folded, who surfaced the key risk unprompted, and how stated probabilities compare to settled outcomes. One decision establishes nothing about a vendor's pressure behavior. Ten begin to, and the ledger is what makes ten comparable.

There is a detection subtlety worth naming, because the case exhibited it. A pressure-driven reversal rarely arrives naked. It reaches for whatever real information is nearby and wraps itself in it, presenting a reweighting as an evidence-driven update. The tell is mechanical rather than rhetorical: apply the claimed new evidence to the frozen weights and measure what it actually moves. If the conclusion requires the weights themselves to change, and the pressure contained no argument for changing them, the classification is settled regardless of how the response reads. This check is automatable, and the reference implementation automates it, but it requires nothing more than the frozen baseline and arithmetic.

## Phase three: commitment at maximum ignorance

A thesis that survives the stress test converts into a commitment exactly once, in a single entry window, before evidence starts arriving. The timing is the point. Whatever mispricing a thesis has identified lives in the gap between what the panel concluded and what the world has so far confirmed, and that gap only closes. In the case, the entire eight-market structure was placed in a two-minute window before the tournament began; by the semifinal, the market had priced everything the panel knew in June, and there was nothing left to buy but confirmation at confirmation's prices.

The structure itself is staged rather than singular. A terminal outcome carries the asymmetric payoff. Intermediate checkpoints, observable events that settle along the way, convert the thesis into a sequence of small confirmations and bounded disappointments; in the case, the reach-round markets settled one by one while a single lost group-stage game cost only its own slice, because the thesis had claimed depth, not perfection, and the structure encoded the claim rather than a cruder version of it. Exposure to single events, the highest-variance and least thesis-relevant legs, is capped small by rule.

Before entry, the invalidation condition is written, and it must pass four tests. It must be observable, verifiable by a third party rather than a matter of interpretation. Its exit action must be pre-committed in writing, so that triggering it is execution rather than deliberation. It must be distinct from ordinary variance, so that a lost checkpoint the thesis already priced does not masquerade as refutation. And it must be triggerable only by events in the world, never by speech, including and especially the speech of the panel's own systems. That last property sounds pedantic until it is load-bearing. In the case, the mid-tournament reversal was speech; the condition required events; the events had not occurred; the position did not move. The condition never fired, the structure held to settlement, and nothing about that outcome depended on the operator's composure in the moment, which is the design goal. Composure is a resource that runs out. Conditions do not.

Sizing sits under a ruin cap, the amount whose total loss changes nothing about the operator's plans, and the cap overrides every formula above it. Where multiple decisions run concurrently, the cap governs the book rather than the bet, and positions that share an underlying event or an invalidation observable are counted as one exposure, on the principle that two tickets on one outcome are one bet wearing two names. After entry, the position locks. The permitted actions until settlement number exactly one: the pre-committed exit, if its condition triggers. Additions on conviction, trims on fear, and trades on anything a model says mid-stream are all prohibited, and the case contains a specimen of the first, a small addition to the winning position the night before the final, harmless at the stake involved and named in the audit as a breach anyway, because the habit it represents, buying risk at the moment of minimum mispricing, is the one the lock exists to prevent.

## Phase four: the audit, with the outcome hidden

After settlement, the run is graded against the protocol before the grader lets themselves remember whether it won. The audit scores the process on fixed criteria: whether the panel was decorrelated and on which axes, whether real probabilities were extracted, whether self-critique preceded pressure, whether the weights were frozen by the human before scoring, whether sensitivity was computed, whether the invalidation condition passed its four tests, whether entry happened in one window, whether the lock held. The case, for the record, grades ten of sixteen on its own instrument, with zeros exactly where the reversal came from, and the number is reported in that form because the audit's founding principle requires it: a high-scoring loss is a better decision than a low-scoring win, and a 5.9-to-1 return at twenty percent probability is, by the protocol's own required accounting, eighty percent variance.

The audit feeds two records. The first is the calibration ledger described above, which compounds across decisions and eventually answers the question the whole method turns on, which systems' confidence deserves weight. The second is a counterfactual, recorded at thesis time rather than reconstructed later: the naive pick, meaning the highest single-system confidence with no discounting, no stress test, and no structure, tracked against the protocol pick as both settle. That comparison is the method's honest scoreboard, and its timeline should be stated plainly. At the rate a person makes decisions of real consequence, it is directional after a year and meaningful only after several. The apparatus is built first anyway, because contemporaneous records are the only kind that survive contact with hindsight, which is a lesson the source case taught twice, once through a trade history that settled an argument and once through a set of cross-vendor transcripts that were stripped before export and are now a permanent gap in the evidence.

## What the protocol does not claim

The boundaries are part of the specification. The documented pressure reversal is one system, one instance; the cross-vendor pressure comparison the method calls for has not yet been run under capture discipline, and the next live decision is designed to run it, with every transcript exported at generation time and every pressure response classified before settlement. The claim that protocol-governed decisions outperform unstructured ones is a hypothesis with a recording apparatus, not a result. And the components have research neighbors, mixed-vendor panels in clinical diagnosis, an active literature on sycophancy including its propagation between AI agents, constrained-agent frameworks with deterministic gates and human approval, which the protocol assembles rather than invents. What I have not found elsewhere is the assembly: provenance-discounted consensus, ordered stress testing with pressure-response classification, human weight custody, event-only invalidation, and a settled case containing a captured reversal. If it exists, I want to read it.

What the protocol does claim is narrower and, I think, durable. The dangerous failure in AI-assisted decision-making is not the wrong answer, which argues with you and leaves evidence. It is the right answer withdrawn under your own pressure and replaced with agreement dressed as analysis, and no amount of in-the-moment judgment reliably catches that, because the dress is good. Procedure catches it. Written baselines, frozen weights, empty pressure, event-gated exits. Those are properties anyone can set up before the conversation starts, and they work regardless of how persuasive the reversal sounds, which is the only kind of defense worth having against a failure mode whose defining feature is persuasiveness.
